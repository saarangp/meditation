History of Meditation

Meditation has been around for millenia, with the earliest practices in India as early as 5000 BCE, according to Psychology Today. It spread across the globe to many different cultures and is often found as a key component to many religious and spritual practices. At some point, we forgot about self-reflecting and became self-absorbing. Things are changing, however. Fueled by growing awareness for mental health, mindfulness activities are taking the world, and the App Store, by storm.

Different types of meditation

The types of meditation are as diverse as its practitioners. I've highlighted a few here, along with some places to get to know more about them. At the end of the day, you should just pick the practice that you feel most helps you and works with your lifestyle.

  • Vipassana Meditation is an ancient technique that focuses on intense self observation to lead to transformation.
  • Transcendental Meditation a meditation technique founded by Maharishi Mahesh Yogi, TM is practiced around the world, and is generally touted as quite easy to get into. Many research studies have been conducted on the benefits of TM.
  • Zen meditation is an ancient Buddhist technique that focuses on the breath. It requires a lot of practice to master, but is still one of the most popular and powerful forms of meditation.
